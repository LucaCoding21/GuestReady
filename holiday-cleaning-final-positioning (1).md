## A3: The 6 Core Objections (And How to Pre-Empt Them)

Research across cleaning industry surveys reveals these objections:

### Objection 1: "My house is too dirty/messy"
- **Psychology:** Shame, fear of judgment
- **Reality:** 70%+ of people feel this way
- **Pre-emption:** "We've seen it allâ€”no judgment, just results"
- **In Messaging:** Never use words like "dirty" or "messy"â€”use "busy" and "ready for guests"

### Objection 2: "I'm worried about strangers in my home"
- **Psychology:** Vulnerability, safety concerns
- **Reality:** #1 barrier to hiring cleaning services
- **Pre-emption:** "Background-checked, insured, professional cleaners"
- **In Messaging:** Lead with trust signals, not cleaning descriptions

### Objection 3: "It's too expensive / I can do it myself"
- **Psychology:** Value perception, self-reliance identity
- **Reality:** People who say this often CAN'T do it themselves (no time)
- **Pre-emption:** Frame as time purchase, not cleaning purchase
- **In Messaging:** "Get 4 hours back" not "Get your house cleaned"

### Objection 4: "What if they don't do a good job?"
- **Psychology:** Quality uncertainty, past bad experiences
- **Reality:** 40% have had bad cleaning experiences
- **Pre-emption:** Guarantee + specific checklist
- **In Messaging:** "Guest-ready guaranteed or we come back free"

### Objection 5: "I don't know if I can trust reviews/this company"
- **Psychology:** Skepticism of marketing claims
- **Reality:** 92% trust peer recommendations over ads
- **Pre-emption:** Social proof from verifiable sources
- **In Messaging:** Use TaskRabbit ratings as borrowed credibility

### Objection 6: "I'll think about it / do it later"
- **Psychology:** Procrastination, decision avoidance
- **Reality:** 60%+ of holiday hosts are procrastinators
- **Pre-emption:** Real urgency + loss framing
- **In Messaging:** "Only 6 spots left before Christmas"
